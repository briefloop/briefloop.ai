The Loop

The full journey of a claim

From source to human sign-off, every claim leaves a trail on the deterministic control plane. Claims the gate refuses go back into the repair loop — they do not sneak into the delivery bundle.

ledger-backed claim → delivery gate-blocked → repair loop solid nodes = deterministic control, dashed = AI drafting
Governance

Corporate governance, for your AI

AI drafts and proposes. BriefLoop's deterministic control layer audits and keeps the books. Separation of duties is what makes the output trustworthy.

Traditional AI writing (black box)

  • Unverifiable data: after a few revision rounds, the true origin of a number is often impossible to re-check.
  • Errors compound: one small misreading in a draft gets polished into a confident, wrong conclusion.
  • Feedback evaporates: reviewer preferences live in someone's head; the model forgets them on the next run.
  • Expensive review: with no evidence trail, review teams re-verify source documents by hand, every time.

BriefLoop chain of custody (auditable)

  • Ledger-registered facts: every material claim and number in the deliverable has coordinates in the Claim Ledger.
  • Deterministic quality gates: Python logic enforces freshness and source-integrity gates — the model cannot grade its own homework.
  • Approved preferences persist: human-approved writing preferences become durable memory with contamination-safe rollback records.
  • One delivery-truth record: delivery status comes only from finalize_report and the completion projection. A failed reader-clean never promotes the bundle — file existence is not delivery.
Scenarios

Built for buyers who bear consequences

In business settings where mistakes have consequences, we help humans hold the last line.

Investor Relations (IR)

Pain: quarterly and disclosure data has tangled origins; answering analyst or reviewer follow-ups means hunting for the original evidence.

BriefLoop: an auto-generated Source Map with traceable coordinates lets disclosure data snap back to the original page and context.

Strategy & Investment Research

Pain: weekly competitor tracking changes constantly; multi-round editing lets unverified numbers slip in.

BriefLoop: a strict authority layer and multi-agent topology lock core claims so polish never mutates facts.

Compliance & Review

Pain: manually re-verifying the sources behind AI drafts is expensive; stale data and missing evidence are hard to catch.

BriefLoop: deterministic gates flag stale or unsupported statements automatically, cutting review and correction cost.

Executive Briefings

Pain: multi-source briefs drift; AI conclusions sound absolute without attribution to back them.

BriefLoop: strict human/machine separation — only human-approved feedback enters memory as preference, with a clear, controllable baseline.

Governance Boundary

Human responsibility statement

No automated AI system should — or can — take over human responsibility for decisions and sign-off. BriefLoop does not approve delivery for you; it gives you an evidence chain that reduces the risk of missing something — traceability, not semantic proof.

AI agents draft, analyze, propose (drafting layer)
Control logic (Python) audits, records, enforces gates (audit layer)
Human decision-maker signs, approves, bears responsibility (judgment layer)
"When data conflicts or goes missing, the system exposes the trail and supports fast trace-back."

Developers

Built for developers and agents

BriefLoop fits both hacker workflows and enterprise deployment. Run it standalone from the command line, or orchestrate it from an AI editor with WorkBuddy — the final brief still has to satisfy the quality contract.

Deterministic CLI

Inspect the current stage, missing files, blockers, and audit reports from the command line. Delivery status comes only from the single delivery-truth record, finalize_report.json.

WorkBuddy / Claude Code

Say "run my industry weekly" in WorkBuddy, or kick off a run from Claude Code with briefloop run --workspace ~/my-workspace.

bash — briefloop
$ pipx install briefloop
$ briefloop onboard
# guided Q&A captures industry, purpose, and reader preferences into onboarding.json
$ briefloop init ~/my-workspace --from-onboarding onboarding.json
$ briefloop run --workspace ~/my-workspace
[info] Claim Ledger frozen → output/intermediate/claim_ledger.json
[gate] auditor quality gates passed → stage-complete
[finalize] reader-clean passed → delivery_promotion: "promoted"
[done] Delivery bundle: output/delivery/brief.md · delivery truth in finalize_report.json
